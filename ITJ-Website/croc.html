    About the Philippine Crocodile

    a critically endangered freshwater species endemic to the Philippines, known as one of the world's rarest reptiles with fewer than 140 left in the wild

    Primary Threats
    Habitat loss
    Hunting
    Disease
    Pollution
    Habitat
    marshes, rivers, and lakes in Northern Luzon and Mindanao. Saltwater crocodiles inhabit coastal waters, mangrove forests, and large rivers, particularly in Palawan.
