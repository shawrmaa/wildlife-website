    • Philippine Eagle

    Pithecophaga jefferyi
    Conservation Status: Critically Endangered
    Population: 400-500
    About the Philippine Eagle

    The Philippine Eagle is a large bird of prey found only in the Philippines. It is critically endangered due to habitat loss and hunting.

    Primary Threats
    Habitat loss
    Deforestation
    Hunting
    Shooting: Airgun injuries
    Habitat
    dense tropical rainforests, primarily in Mindanao, Luzon, Samar, and Leyte.
    Population
    400-500
    Conservation
    • Protection programs:(PEF)
    • Community-based conservation
    • Research and Monitoring
    • Habitat restoration
